# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and fellow engineers in embedded systems / robotics. They arrive from LinkedIn or a resume link, skim in under 60 seconds, and decide whether Uriah is worth a callback. Secondary: colleagues and collaborators checking his work.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Uriah Villa — MCU Applications Engineer II at Microchip, MS in Engineering with Distinction (4.0 GPA), robotics/embedded specialist. The site must make his hardware-software depth legible fast: NASA Psyche capstone, TinyML edge-AI, motor control, PCB design. Success = a visitor leaves convinced this person ships real hardware.

## Brand Personality

Precise, engineered, quietly confident. The voice of a datasheet written by someone who loves the craft — technical material presented with instrument-panel clarity. Not flashy; exact. Colors: electric blue + deep violet (user-committed). Light and dark modes (user-committed).

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing-page template (hero metric, gradient text, icon-card grids)
- Web-developer portfolio clichés (emoji waves, "I build things for the web")
- Editorial-magazine affectation (display serifs, italic drop caps) — wrong register for an embedded engineer
- Anything a visitor could identify as AI-generated at a glance

## Design Principles

1. **Show the bench.** Circuit traces, waveforms, kinematics — his actual material is the site's imagery. Precise geometric diagrams, never decorative doodles.
2. **Spec-sheet honesty.** Real numbers carry weight: ±2°, 500+ cycles, 36° slopes, 10+ hrs/week saved. Surface them as data, not marketing copy.
3. **Instrument-panel hierarchy.** One dominant idea per fold. Mono type is his literal material (firmware), used for data/labels — never as costume.
4. **Fast is the aesthetic.** Single file, no framework, sub-second load. The site itself demonstrates embedded discipline.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1 in both themes. `prefers-reduced-motion` honored (crossfades instead of movement). Semantic HTML, keyboard navigable, visible focus states.
